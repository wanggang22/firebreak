A non-custodial liquidation firewall for stablecoin-native lending on Arc. A borrower signs an on-chain Mandate (health trigger, spend cap, action whitelist); an autonomous keeper watches the loan and, before liquidators can move, executes the cheapest bounded rescue in one atomic transaction. Below is a replay of a real rescue executed on Arc testnet — every number and the transaction are on-chain and verifiable. Claude ranks the vetted paths; sizing, spend cap, and the action whitelist stay deterministic, so the model can only pick a bounds-checked path.

Everything up to here is a replay of a transaction that really executed on Arc. The three capabilities below are built and tested but not deployed to testnet — the deployed Mandate predates them. They are labelled that way rather than shown as chain proof.

Cross-chain reserve Gateway verified

TOP-UP repays from a prepaid reserve — and the rescue you just replayed spent 0.94 of the 1.00 that was in it. Look at what the mandate holds now:

reserve before that rescue1.00 USDC
spent by the TOP-UP0.94 USDC
reserve today, on Arc0.06 USDC

One rescue nearly emptied the magazine. The next drift finds the cheapest path unavailable, and the position takes a worse one. Parking enough idle USDC on Arc to survive months of this — against a liquidation that may never come — is a bad trade.

So the reserve lives in the borrower's Circle Unified Balance, across any chain they already hold USDC on, and the keeper refills Arc when it drops below a floor — in a second loop, never inside a rescue, so rescues stay atomic.

layerborrower signskeeper may
Arcregister(Terms)move collateral
CircleaddDelegate(USDC)pull reserve

Non-custodial on both layers — revoking either one alone stops the keeper.

A second protocol 12 tests

Every integration claims to be protocol-agnostic. With one adapter — written by us, shaped like our own interface — that claim is self-certified. So we wrote a second pool designed to break it.

poolhow it keeps score
MiniLendraw amounts
ScaledLendshares + accruing index

Share-based collateral and index-accrued debt is how Aave and Compound actually work, and it is what breaks naive integrations — a keeper assuming collateralOf returns a stored number sizes the rescue wrong. The same Mandate bytecode guards both.

It also exposes a risk the first pool cannot express. The index rises every second, so health decays with the market perfectly still:

price before / after1.08 → 1.08
health factorfalls anyway

Price drift is a risk that may never arrive. Interest drift is arithmetic, and it never stops. test_HealthDecaysWithAStillMarket

Keeper economics 5 tests

An autonomous keeper costs money to run, but a careless fee turns it against the borrower it guards. The shape matters more than the number.

feeflat, fixed at signing
paidonly after a rescue succeeds
reserve too lowrescue still happens

A percentage would pay more for bigger rescues and quietly reward the most expensive viable path. Flat leaves the keeper indifferent to size, so choosing the cheapest path that works costs it nothing.

It cannot farm frequency either: a rescue is reachable only below the trigger and must lift health past it — pushing the position out of the band it would have to re-enter to bill again. Keeper revenue tracks how often the market threatens the position, not anything the keeper decides.

An underfunded reserve costs the keeper its fee, never the borrower the rescue.
