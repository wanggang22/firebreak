■ FIREBREAK
The liquidation firewall for stablecoin-native lending on Arc.
non-custodial · autonomous · settled in USDC
the problem
Liquidation doesn't flash-crash.
It drifts.
On a stablecoin-native chain your collateral is FX and tokenized RWAs. They don't gap down forty percent in a candle — they slide. The euro moves two cents. An RWA re-prices. Interest accrues.
Health factor walks slowly toward the line, and a liquidator has been waiting for hours.
and your options are bad
A 5–10% penalty — for something a 0.94 USDC top-up would have fixed.
✕ Watch it yourself

Nobody is checking their health factor at 3am. Drift happens while you sleep.

✕ Give a bot your keys

Now something else holds your collateral, and can do anything with it.

Automation you can't bound isn't safety. It's a different risk.
the fix
The borrower signs an on-chain Mandate.

The contract re-checks every bound at execution. The keeper never holds your funds — and can't exceed the cap, take an un-whitelisted action, or touch a healthy position.
where the AI goes — and where it must not
The LLM ranks. Deterministic code sizes and bounds-checks.

deterministic core

Builds every allowed path. Sizes each to restore health. Prices it. Drops anything breaching the cap.

→ vetted, executable candidates

claude ranks — only that set

A strict tool enum of candidate ids. It writes the borrower's memo. Any error or out-of-set pick falls back to the cheapest path.

→ never unbounded, never stalled

Its only freedom is which safe path — never whether it's safe. Sizing is arithmetic, and arithmetic stays in code.
